Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Operational Excellence Framework

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Operational Philosophy

Socinga Africa Mining is committed to operational excellence in every facet of its business. This document establishes the strategic operational framework that guides day-to-day activities, performance management, continuous improvement, and stakeholder engagement across all pillars.

3. Performance Management

The Company employs a Balanced Scorecard methodology across four perspectives:

  1. Financial: Revenue growth, cost discipline, ROIC, and free cash flow generation.
  2. Customer/Stakeholder: Investor confidence metrics, community satisfaction indices, and regulatory compliance scores.
  3. Internal Processes: Production efficiency, safety performance, environmental compliance, and project delivery timelines.
  4. Learning & Growth: Training hours per employee, leadership pipeline depth, innovation metrics, and technology adoption rates.

4. Supply Chain & Procurement

  • All procurement shall prioritise local and regional suppliers where quality and price are competitive.
  • Strategic inventory holdings for critical spares (minimum 90-day supply for mission-critical equipment).
  • Vendor performance reviews conducted quarterly using cost, quality, delivery, and safety (CQDS) metrics.
  • Long-term framework agreements for high-value consumables (diesel, chemicals, grinding media).

5. Technology & Innovation

The Company shall adopt technology solutions that enhance safety, efficiency, and decision-making:

  • Fleet management systems with GPS tracking and real-time utilisation dashboards.
  • Automated grade-control sampling and on-belt analysis for processing optimisation.
  • The SAM Dossier platform for centralised document management, investor reporting, and boardroom collaboration.
  • Drone-based aerial surveys for pit mapping, stockpile measurement, and environmental monitoring.

6. Continuous Improvement

All divisions shall participate in a structured continuous improvement programme based on Lean Six Sigma principles. Each divisional head shall identify a minimum of two improvement projects per quarter, with measured impact on cost, safety, or throughput.
