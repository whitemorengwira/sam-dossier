Establishment Policy

Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Group Holding Company

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Preamble

This Establishment Policy sets forth the founding charter, governance framework, and operational mandate of Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd ("the Company"), a private holding entity registered under the laws of the Republic of South Africa. The Company serves as the principal vehicle through which the Socinga Africa ecosystem deploys capital, manages mineral assets, and coordinates cross-border mining operations across the African continent.

The board of directors has resolved that this policy shall serve as the foundational governance instrument from which all subsidiary policies, operational procedures, and strategic mandates derive their authority.

3. Vision & Mission

Vision: To be Africa's most trusted and responsible mining investment group, delivering institutional-grade returns whilst uplifting communities and preserving ecological heritage.

Mission: Socinga Africa Mining acquires, develops, and operates de-risked mineral assets through a vertically integrated model that prioritises safety, environmental stewardship, community benefit, and shareholder value creation.

4. Founding Principles

  1. Sovereign Compliance: The Company shall at all times operate in full compliance with the mining, environmental, labour, and corporate governance laws of each jurisdiction in which it holds interests.
  2. Fiduciary Duty: Directors and officers owe an unwavering fiduciary duty to the Company and its shareholders, prioritising long-term value over short-term gains.
  3. Community Partnership: Every mining operation shall maintain a Community Development Agreement that ensures measurable social upliftment, including education, healthcare, and infrastructure programmes.
  4. Environmental Guardianship: The Company commits to progressive mine rehabilitation, carbon-offset initiatives, and adherence to or exceeding the Equator Principles.
  5. Transparency: All material decisions, financial positions, and operational metrics shall be disclosed to stakeholders in a timely, accurate, and comprehensible manner.

6. Operational Scope

The Company's operational mandate encompasses:

  • Acquisition and development of gold, platinum-group metals, and base-metal mining concessions across Southern and East Africa.
  • Establishment and management of processing plants, including stamp mills, ball mills, CIP/CIL circuits, and tailings reprocessing facilities.
  • Deployment of geological survey, exploration, and feasibility study programmes.
  • Creation and administration of community benefit-sharing mechanisms and social investment vehicles.
  • Management of downstream value chains including refining partnerships, off-take agreements, and commodity trading desks.

7. Amendments

This policy may only be amended by a resolution of the Board of Directors passed by a two-thirds majority at a duly convened meeting. Any proposed amendment must be circulated to all directors no fewer than fourteen (14) business days prior to the meeting at which it is to be considered.
