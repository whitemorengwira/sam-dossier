Introduction

Socinga Africa desires to be a duly business-constituted juristic body in terms of the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

Compliant with the King V Code, the business will establish its activities directed mainly by its written rules, written norms, written standards, and written policies, all aimed at ensuring it conducts all its business separately from its founding owners.

This policy will be applied in conjunction with Socinga Africa Board of Directors and Senior Executive Management decisions from time to time.

Policy Provision

  • This policy handles Socinga Africa business establishment rules.
  • This policy will be utilised as a guide to the Board, Senior Management, and lower management on the minimum regulations to which the company commits.
  • Business Company to initiate its activities in the following business industries: Mineral Asset Investment, Broadcast creative production, Community Development Infrastructure, Long-term Insurance.

Businesses are established and grouped into the following business pillars: Funeral cover Insurance, Creative Studios, Mining, and Foundation.

Establishment of Socinga Africa as a Juristic Body

Socinga Africa, as a Business Company on its own separate identity, is herein established in operations, recognising all founding documents, founding directives, founding members, founding board, founding agreements, and founding policies.

Governance of the Company

The company is governed by the Board, in which its highest decision-making rests with executive function as an executing authority.

The company is managed by senior management comprising the Managing Director, the Chief Financial Officer, the Chief Executive Officer, and the Director of Corporate Services for day-to-day decisions.

Transitional Arrangements Window

Socinga Africa emerges from the support and establishment by Ms Jabulile Dladla, one of the Directors of Socinga Mgabadeli Pty Ltd, from which the fiduciary duty supports in terms of major resources stem.

It is a critical provision to allow the business to grow from establishment early stages to a fully functional phase as a micro-level business transitioning with easy and simple day-to-day decision making, giving the security required by individual founders in recognition of inherent risks.

This business shall have a highest decision-making body transitional window shared interchangeably between the powers of the Board and Ms Jabulile Dladla, whose decisions take precedence until such time that, through a Board meeting, the power is handed over, relinquished, and surrendered per the provisions of that meeting.

  • Instructions by Senior Management within their line function.
  • Instructions from lower management hierarchy within the powers of their appointment.

Jurisdictional Mandate of Business Operations

Socinga Africa is established as a Pan-African business in nature and takes the form of being a company operating in the entire Continent of Africa and follows its projects related to Africa anywhere in the Diaspora. The seat of administration office is in Roodepoort, South Africa. Any established office anywhere else is regarded as a satellite office.

Transgression & Validation

It is herein declared that any person failing to observe these rules by intention or omission is committing a dismissible offence.

  • Documents (Service Contracts, MOAs, MOUs) signed by the Managing Director or Ms Jabulile Dladla on the basis of the Transitional window clause while it subsists and presented to the board are valid.
  • In the event of an emergency, any instruction signed and issued by a CEO or his/her delegate is valid if brought to the attention of the board, or the most senior manager available.
  • No policy is valid until signed by two (2) senior managers and ratified by the chairperson with a company stamp and oath affirming validity.
