Mining Strategy Addendum

Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Chikonga Mine Development Specifics

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Purpose

This Addendum supplements the Mining Strategic Policy with site-specific operational parameters for the Chikonga Mine concession located in Mutare District, Manicaland Province, Zimbabwe. It addresses geological context, mine plan specifics, processing flowsheet design, and community integration targets not covered in the parent policy.

2. Geological Summary

The Chikonga concession lies within the Mutare Greenstone Belt, characterised by:

  • Archaean metavolcanic and metasedimentary sequences hosting orogenic gold mineralisation.
  • Primary gold occurrence in quartz veins within shear zones striking NE–SW with moderate dips.
  • Historical artisanal workings confirming gold grades of 5–25 g/t in vein material.
  • Indicated Resource (JORC 2012): 850,000 tonnes at 4.2 g/t Au for approximately 115,000 contained ounces.
  • Inferred Resource: 1,200,000 tonnes at 3.1 g/t Au for approximately 120,000 contained ounces.

4. Processing Flowsheet

  1. Crushing: Primary jaw crusher → secondary cone crusher to −12mm.
  2. Milling: Ball mill in closed circuit with hydrocyclone classification to 80% passing 75µm.
  3. Gravity Separation: Knelson concentrator recovering coarse free gold (est. 35% of total recovery).
  4. Leaching: Carbon-in-pulp (CIP) circuit with 24-hour residence time, NaCN concentration 350 ppm.
  5. Elution & Smelting: AARL elution, electrowinning, and on-site doré smelting.
  6. Tailings Management: Lined tailings storage facility with cyanide detoxification and monitoring.

5. Community Integration Targets

  • 80% of unskilled and semi-skilled labour sourced from communities within 30 km radius.
  • 5% of net profit allocated to the Chikonga Community Development Trust annually.
  • Construction of a community clinic and borehole water supply within Year 1.
  • Artisanal Mining Formalisation Programme offering technical training and equipment to 200 local miners.
