Structural Policy

Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Organisational Architecture

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Purpose

This Structural Policy defines the organisational architecture, departmental hierarchy, reporting lines, and decision-making authority of Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd. It gives effect to the Establishment Policy (Ref: 01/0000/2026) and provides the structural framework within which all subsidiary policies operate.

2. Organisational Tiers

The Company operates across four organisational tiers:

TierLevelDescription
1Board of DirectorsStrategic oversight, fiduciary governance, and appointment of executive management.
2Executive Committee (EXCO)Day-to-day management, operational decisions, and policy implementation. Comprises CEO, MD, and CFO.
3Divisional HeadsOperational leadership of the five core pillars: Mining, Insurance, Creative, Foundation, and Group Services.
4Operational StaffTechnical, administrative, and field personnel executing against divisional mandates.

3. Five Pillar Structure

The Socinga Africa ecosystem is organised into five strategic pillars, each with distinct mandates and accountability frameworks:

  1. Mining Pillar: Gold, PGM, and base-metal extraction across Southern and East Africa. Responsible for geological exploration, mine development, processing, and off-take management.
  2. Insurance Pillar: Risk underwriting, asset protection, and financial hedging for mining operations. Includes self-insurance vehicles and third-party broker relationships.
  3. Creative Pillar: Brand management, investor communications, digital presence, and marketing strategy. Encompasses the SAM Dossier platform and all public-facing digital assets.
  4. Foundation Pillar: Community development, social investment, education programmes, and CSI initiatives linked to mining licence obligations.
  5. Group Services: Shared corporate functions including legal, compliance, human resources, IT infrastructure, and financial administration.

4. Reporting Lines

The following reporting hierarchy governs the flow of information and accountability:

  • Divisional Heads report to the Managing Director.
  • The Managing Director reports to the Chief Executive Officer.
  • The Chief Financial Officer reports to the Board on all financial matters and to the CEO on operational matters.
  • The Chairperson presides over Board proceedings and liaises with the CEO between meetings.

6. Policy Review

This Structural Policy shall be reviewed annually by the Board. Amendments require a two-thirds majority and must be circulated fourteen (14) business days before the meeting at which they are tabled.
