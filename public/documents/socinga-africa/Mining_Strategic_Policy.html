Mining Strategic Policy

Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Mineral Extraction & Development Strategy

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Strategic Vision

This policy articulates the mining strategy for Socinga Africa Mining, positioning the Company as a mid-tier gold producer with a clear pathway to 50,000 oz/year production within five years. The strategy centres on the Chikonga Mine concession in Mutare, Zimbabwe, as the anchor asset, supplemented by exploration-stage concessions in Mozambique, Zambia, Ghana, and Malawi.

3. Operational Model

The Company shall employ a phased, capital-efficient mining model:

  1. Phase 1 — Semi-Mechanised: Gravity separation, stamp milling, and CIP circuit. Target: 10–15,000 oz/yr at sub-US$900/oz AISC.
  2. Phase 2 — Mechanised Expansion: CIL plant, underground development, and expanded tailings reprocessing. Target: 25–35,000 oz/yr.
  3. Phase 3 — Full Industrialisation: Multi-shaft operation, regional processing hub, and downstream refining partnership. Target: 50,000 oz/yr.

4. Geological Standards

All mineral resource and reserve estimates shall conform to the JORC Code (2012) or SAMREC Code. Independent Competent Persons shall be engaged for all public resource statements. Exploration programmes shall follow best-practice sampling, assaying (fire assay + ICP-OES), and quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC) protocols.

5. Off-Take Strategy

Gold production shall be sold through a combination of:

  • Forward-sale contracts with approved LBMA refiners (maximum 60% of annual production).
  • Spot market sales through accredited bullion dealers.
  • Strategic reserve holdings (minimum 10% of annual production retained as gold inventory).
