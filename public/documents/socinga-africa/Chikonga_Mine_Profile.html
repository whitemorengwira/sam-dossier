Executive Summary

Chikonga Mine is a subsidiary of Hilltouch Investments, an indigenous gold mining entity that is wholly owned by its Directors Mr. Lufeyi Shato & Mrs. Joyce Kujenga. Established in 2005, it has grown in leaps and bounds from humble beginnings to become Manicaland’s 3rd largest producer of the yellow mineral.

Chikonga Mine is located 20km off Mutare CBD along the Mutare-Harare highway. The mine boasts a 50-staff compliment alongside casual workers. The 45-hectare property comprises four 10-hectare registered claims.

All four claims are currently or have previously been mined on a small scale for gold with the first recorded production in 1959. Good and better standards of mining are presently being conducted by Hilltouch on multiple, steeply dipping, parallel narrow reefs (shear zones) stacked across a 350-meter wide, 800-meter+ structural corridor.

Improved gold grades averaged 15g/t, 18g/t, and 25g/t in 2019, 2020, and 2021 respectively.

Mineralized reefs/shear zones occur as siliceous mica schist and silicified andesite typically hosting bands of fine-grained grey and black quartz with disseminated pyrrhotite, pyrite, and chalcopyrite. Visible gold occasionally occurs in white and glassy quartz lenses up to 30 centimeters thick within the shear zones. High-grade gold mineralization is controlled by distinct plunging shoots, though the exact plunge direction remains untested below 60 meters.
