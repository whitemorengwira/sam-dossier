Ecosystem Policies

Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Cross-Pillar Governance Standards

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Scope

These Ecosystem Policies govern the conduct, inter-pillar coordination, and shared standards applicable across all divisions of the Socinga Africa Mining ecosystem. They complement the Establishment Policy and Structural Policy by prescribing behavioural, operational, and compliance norms that every team member, contractor, and partner must observe.

2. Code of Conduct

Every individual engaged by or representing the Company shall:

  • Act with integrity, honesty, and respect in all professional interactions.
  • Avoid conflicts of interest; declare any actual or perceived conflicts to the compliance officer immediately.
  • Refrain from accepting gifts, gratuities, or inducements that could compromise objectivity.
  • Protect confidential information and intellectual property of the Company and its partners.
  • Report any suspected fraud, corruption, or unethical behaviour through the designated whistleblower channel.

3. Environmental, Social & Governance (ESG) Standards

The Company commits to:

  1. Environmental: Compliance with the National Environmental Management Act (NEMA), mine closure and rehabilitation planning from day one, water stewardship, and carbon-footprint reduction targets aligned with Paris Agreement commitments.
  2. Social: Fair wages, safe working conditions, community engagement programmes, preferential local procurement, and zero tolerance for child or forced labour.
  3. Governance: Board independence, transparent financial reporting, anti-bribery and anti-corruption controls, and regular third-party governance audits.

4. Health, Safety & Environment (HSE)

All mining and processing operations shall maintain an HSE management system certified to ISO 45001 (Occupational Health & Safety) and ISO 14001 (Environmental Management). Key requirements:

  • Zero-harm culture: every incident, near-miss, and hazard shall be reported and investigated.
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) is mandatory on all operational sites.
  • Emergency response plans shall be documented, drilled quarterly, and updated annually.
  • Occupational health surveillance for all employees exposed to silica dust, noise, or hazardous chemicals.

5. Data Protection & Information Security

The Company processes personal data in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) and applicable international data protection regulations. All digital systems shall implement:

  • Multi-factor authentication for access to financial, geological, and personnel systems.
  • Encrypted data storage and transmission (AES-256 minimum).
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments (biannually).
  • Data retention and destruction schedules aligned with regulatory requirements.

6. Inter-Pillar Collaboration

Each pillar shall appoint a Liaison Officer responsible for cross-pillar coordination. Monthly Ecosystem Coordination Committee (ECC) meetings shall address:

  • Shared resource planning and allocation.
  • Cross-functional project status updates.
  • Risk escalation and mitigation strategies.
  • Joint procurement opportunities.
