Financial Policy

Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd — Financial Governance Framework

Effective Date: 1 January 2026 • Board Approved

1. Purpose

This Financial Policy establishes the fiscal governance framework for Socinga Africa Mining (Pty) Ltd and its subsidiaries. It gives effect to the Establishment Policy (Ref: 01/0000/2026) and prescribes the standards, controls, and procedures governing all financial operations, reporting, and risk management activities.

2. Financial Authority

The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) is the principal financial authority and is responsible for:

  • Preparation and presentation of annual budgets to the Board.
  • Management of cash flow, treasury functions, and banking relationships.
  • Ensuring statutory compliance with the South African Revenue Service (SARS), Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC), and other fiscal authorities.
  • Oversight of internal and external audit processes.
  • Implementation of International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) across all reporting entities.

4. Revenue Management

All revenue from mining operations, off-take agreements, refining proceeds, and ancillary income streams shall be:

  1. Deposited into designated Company bank accounts within two (2) business days of receipt.
  2. Reconciled against production records and off-take contracts weekly.
  3. Reported to EXCO monthly in a standardised Revenue & Production Dashboard.
  4. Subject to independent verification by external auditors at minimum annually.

5. Expenditure Controls

  • All expenditure exceeding R100,000 requires two authorised signatories.
  • Procurement above R500,000 requires a minimum of three competitive quotations.
  • No single-source procurement above R250,000 without CEO and CFO joint written approval and documented justification.
  • All expenditure must be supported by a valid purchase order, invoice, and goods-received note.

7. Risk & Insurance

The CFO, in conjunction with the Insurance Pillar, shall maintain adequate insurance coverage for all mining assets, professional indemnity, directors' and officers' liability, and third-party claims. A comprehensive risk register shall be maintained and reviewed quarterly by EXCO.
