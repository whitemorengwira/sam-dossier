Socinga Africa Business Company

Financial Policy

"We commit to Investors – Clients – Employees – Customers – Consumers – Visitors and every person interacting with us that we, as the Board, Senior Executing Management, and founders, shall observe the contents of this policy found by us at all times."

Purpose

  • To give effect to the provisions of Socinga Africa Establishment Policy No. 01/0000/2026.
  • To provide for a financial management framework ensuring there are adequate financial management systems.
  • To provide rules for financial instrument controls and easy observation in compliance with the law.
  • To prevent weak financial management, poor financial reporting, and vulnerabilities.

Introduction & Policy Provision

Socinga Africa desires to be a duly business-constituted juristic body in terms of the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

This policy handles the financial management rules of the entire business, determines accounting procedures and standards, and sets forth management accounting requirements for business operations.

Definition of Financial Management

Financial Management in this company means a trail of legislatively as well as internally recognised required source documents, communications, approvals, requests, and sourcing, which may result in a business incurring a financial commitment or actual buying, or possible loss of cash, assets, inventory, value, or income subject to process and procedure.

Financial Process & Procedure

  • Raising a memo for acquiring goods, services, or assets.
  • Analysing requests on the process of acquiring goods, services, or assets.
  • Obtaining quotations, invoices, clearance of purchase instruments, orders, release of funds.
  • Commitment of cash, receipt of delivery of goods, dispatch of goods, or storage.
  • Initiating conversion of assets into a new item, building new assets, or demolishing assets.
  • Enhancing assets through rehabilitation, refurbishment, or improvement.

Integrity of Financial Process & Segregation of Duties

No single person is allowed to conduct a financial process alone from start to end.

Any transaction must be carried out through clearly defined processes, stages, and segregation of duties for maximum transparency to combat loss (fraud, theft, embezzlement, corruption).

The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) is responsible for ensuring checks and balances are in place, and consolidating business budgets for approval.

Purchasing and Payments

A request is submitted per the prescribed Requisition stating the need in full with quotations. Line senior management assesses and approves. If approved, the CFO is given the purchase order. The Managing Director can release funds; if unavailable, the Chairperson can assist.

Instruments of Monetary Control & Compliance

  • Bank Control: The finances of the company are to be conducted through a registered bank.
  • Cash Handling: Where cash is transacted, disclosure must be made through invoice and deposited within two hours.
  • Petty Cash: Strict register required, not exceeding R10,000.00.
  • CFO Powers: The CFO may carry out audits, verifications, and unannounced inspections.

FINANCIAL DISCLAIMER

Any monies received without the knowledge of the office of the CFO and obtaining proper approval is unauthorised and unlawful. Any investment is authorised if processed and received through the office of the CFO to obtain necessary approval.
